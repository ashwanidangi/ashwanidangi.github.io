01The story

From the sales floor to the marketing P&L.

I started in sales at Justdial, then key accounts, where I learned what a funnel looks like from the inside: every lead has a cost, and every close has a reason. Two years of business analysis and D2C growth at Boult Audio taught me to find those reasons in the data; running marketing at Sotbella meant betting ₹2 Cr on them and standing behind the result.

Today at Battery Smart, India's largest EV battery-swapping network, I own the whole engine: paid, organic, lifecycle, a calling team, and a new Mobility-as-a-Service vertical, plus the P&L it all rolls up to. My favourite part is where strategy meets a spreadsheet; the RCA on what stalled, and the change that fixed it.

03Selected campaigns
CAMPAIGN 01
Battery Smart
Customer Engagement & Marketing Manager
MAR 2025 — PRESENT · DELHI NCR

India's largest EV battery-swapping network. Role expanded from engagement to full marketing ownership.

Problem

Engagement spend was running hot with no clear line to ROI, acquisition and retention lived in separate silos, and an entirely new business line (MaaS) needed a market.

The play
  • Took the marketing P&L — budget across performance media, SEO, CRM and agency retainers, with CAC optimisation and A/B testing deciding where every rupee goes.
  • Rebuilt the acquisition funnel top to bottom: paid campaigns feeding a calling team I lead, on scripts I wrote, with conversion tracked stage by stage.
  • Automated the lifecycle — WhatsApp, SMS, Email, RCS & IVR journeys via Interakt & WebEngage, plus WABA bots including a weather-triggered one for hyper-local sends.
  • Drove retention as its own program, not an afterthought of acquisition.
  • Stood up the MaaS vertical from scoping to go-to-market.
−40%
campaign cost, by moving sends into WhatsApp's 24-hour session window with automated customer journeys.
